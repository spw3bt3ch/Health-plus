Prostate Cancer Risk Assessment

Evaluate your prostate cancer risk factors

No Tests Required

This assessment evaluates your risk based on age, family history, and symptoms. You don't need any prior medical tests or PSA results to complete this assessment. If you have a recent PSA test result, you can include it, but it's optional.

What is PSA?

PSA (Prostate-Specific Antigen) is a protein produced by the prostate. Higher levels may indicate prostate issues, but can also be elevated due to benign conditions.

Normal ranges: Generally 0-4 ng/mL, but can vary by age. This field is optional - you can complete the assessment without it.

Common symptoms to watch for:

  • Frequent urination, especially at night
  • Difficulty starting or stopping urination
  • Weak or interrupted urine flow
  • Pain or burning during urination
  • Blood in urine or semen
  • Persistent pain in lower back, hips, or pelvis
  • Erectile dysfunction
  • Feeling that the bladder doesn't empty completely

Note: These symptoms can also be caused by other conditions like benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) or infections. Early prostate cancer often has no symptoms.
